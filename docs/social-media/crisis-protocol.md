# Crisis & Reputation Management Protocol — LeadHunterIQ

**Purpose**: 3-tier response protocol for social media incidents.
**Governing Order**: CMO — Brand Protection

---

## Tier 1 — Minor Incident

**Examples**: Negative comment, factual error in a post, minor customer complaint on social media.

**Response time**: Within 2 hours

**Protocol**:
1. Acknowledge the comment publicly with a professional tone
2. If factual error in our post: correct immediately with an edit or follow-up reply
3. If customer complaint: reply publicly ("Thanks for flagging — DMing you now"), then take to DM
4. If troll/spam: do not engage, hide or report if platform allows
5. Log the incident in the Social Media Incident Log (Google Sheet)

**Response template** (public):
> "Thanks for bringing this up, [Name]. We take this seriously — let me look into it and get back to you directly. Sending you a DM now."

**Escalation**: None required unless the comment gets 10+ engagements.

---

## Tier 2 — Moderate Incident

**Examples**: Competitor public attack, viral misunderstanding, inaccurate claims about LeadHunterIQ gaining traction, negative thread with engagement.

**Response time**: Within 4 hours

**Protocol**:
1. **Assess**: Screenshot the incident. Determine if it's factual, opinion, or misinformation.
2. **Draft response**: Bhaskar (CEO) responds personally from his LinkedIn/X profile — founder voice carries more authority.
3. **Factual rebuttal only**: Address specific claims with facts. Never attack the competitor or person.
4. **Amplify allies**: If friendly industry contacts can add supportive comments, notify them via Signal Squad WhatsApp group.
5. **Monitor**: Watch for 24 hours. If engagement dies down, no further action.
6. **Document**: Full incident report in Incident Log.

**Response template** (Bhaskar personal):
> "Appreciate the discussion. To clarify: [specific factual correction]. We built LeadHunterIQ specifically for [value proposition]. Happy to discuss further — my DMs are open."

**Escalation**: If engagement exceeds 50+ reactions or media picks it up → Tier 3.

---

## Tier 3 — Severe Incident

**Examples**: Platform policy action (account suspension/delisting), legal threat, data privacy accusation, accusation of scraping/spam (as happened with Apollo on LinkedIn), viral negative press.

**Response time**: Immediate pause + structured response within 12 hours

**Protocol**:
1. **IMMEDIATE**: Pause ALL scheduled posts across all platforms. No new content until resolved.
2. **War room**: Bhaskar + legal counsel assess the situation within 2 hours.
3. **Platform response**: If account action, file appeal immediately with platform support.
4. **Holding statement** (post within 4 hours):
   > "We're aware of [situation]. We take [concern] seriously and are investigating thoroughly. We will provide a full response within [24/48 hours]. Our commitment to [data privacy/compliance/quality] remains our top priority."
5. **Full response**: Only after legal review. Must be factual, professional, and address specific concerns.
6. **Proactive outreach**: Personally notify top 10 clients/prospects about the situation and our response.
7. **Post-incident**: Publish a detailed blog post or LinkedIn article explaining what happened and how we addressed it.

**Never do in Tier 3**:
- Never delete the original incident posts (screenshots exist)
- Never attack the accuser
- Never make promises you can't keep ("we'll never...")
- Never share internal investigation details publicly
- Never admit liability without legal counsel
